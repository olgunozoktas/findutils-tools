Entering into a multi-year business agreement requires more than just a fixed price; it requires a strategy to protect your purchasing power over time. Cumulative inflation can silently erode your profit margins, turning a lucrative deal into a financial burden if the contract lacks proper adjustment mechanisms.

In this guide, you will learn how to identify inflation risks, calculate the real value of future payments, and structure contracts that remain fair for all parties involved.

When a business signs a five-year service agreement at a fixed annual rate, it assumes that its operating costs will remain stable. However, inflation represents the broad increase in prices and the subsequent fall in the purchasing value of money. Cumulative inflation refers to the total increase in price levels over a specific period, compounded year-over-year.
