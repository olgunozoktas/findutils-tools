Relocating for a new career opportunity is an exciting milestone, but a higher salary offer can be deceiving if you don't account for the local economy. To ensure your standard of living improves—or at least stays the same—you must calculate the inflation-adjusted cost of living by comparing your current expenses against the economic realities of your new destination.

When moving between cities or countries, the nominal value of your paycheck matters less than its purchasing power. Purchasing power describes how much "stuff" (housing, food, services) your money can actually buy. If you move from a mid-sized city to a major tech hub with a 20% raise, but the local inflation and cost of living are 30% higher, you have effectively taken a pay cut.
