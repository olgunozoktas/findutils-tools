For decades, the "20% down" rule has been treated as the gold standard of real estate. You’ve likely heard it from parents, banks, and older homeowners: if you don’t have 20% of the purchase price ready in cash, you aren’t ready to buy a home. This advice was rooted in a desire to avoid Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) and secure the lowest possible interest rates. However, in today’s fast-moving housing market, waiting to hit that 20% benchmark can sometimes set you back years, causing you to miss out on equity growth.

The truth is that your ideal down payment strategy depends entirely on your unique financial health, local market conditions, and long-term goals. Whether you are a first-time buyer or a seasoned investor, understanding how different deposit amounts affect your monthly cash flow is essential. By using a [Down Payment Calculator](/en/tools/down-payment-calculator), you can move past the myths and start looking at the real numbers.

The 20% rule originated in an era when mortgage products were less flexible. Today, the landscape is much more diverse. Many government-backed and conventional loans allow for down payments as low as 3% or 3.5%, and some specialized programs even offer 0% down for veterans or rural buyers.
